Pilot studies

Pilot study 1 – Bernstein Conference 2025 (PhD Symposium)

The first pilot study ran as a hackathon during the Bernstein Conference 2025 PhD Symposium, with roughly 80 participants divided in small teams. The teams tackled four questions from the CON²PHYS questionnaire that the participants had previously selected. and worked on them for a full-day hackathon using the shared dataset.

Overall, very little consensus emerged across the teams on any of the four questions. Answers diverged both in terms of chosen analyses and in the resulting quantitative estimates.

This pilot strongly motivated us to continue with the full-scale project. The lack of consensus confirmed that conceptual variability is real and substantial. At the same time, many participants explicitly said that this was the first time they had been forced to think about “consistency” from this conceptual perspective, something that they described as refreshing and thought-provoking. This combination of clear conceptual fragmentation and positive feedback convinced us that CON²PHYS fills a real gap in the field.

Pilot study 2 – Pre-COSYNE Brainhack

The second pilot study is planned as a two-day hackathon at the Pre-COSYNE Brainhack, in collaboration with the International Brain Laboratory (IBL). It will run on March 10–11 and will use the same core ideas as the first pilot, now in a more extended format and with tighter integration into the full CON²PHYS pipeline.

Partecipating in a pilot study and the full CON²PHYS project?

If you participated in one of the two pilot studies, this does not disqualify you from participating in the full CON²PHYS project. On the contrary, you are very much encouraged since you already completed a significant part of the study. The only thing that we require is that you clearly indicate in the questionnaire that you participated in one of the pilot studies and answer the short related questions.
